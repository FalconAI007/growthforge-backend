========================================
GROWTHFORGE MEDIA - CLIENT ONBOARDING PROCESS
========================================

OVERVIEW
========
The GrowthForge Media onboarding process is designed to set up new clients for success from day one. It typically takes 2-3 weeks from contract signing to live campaigns.

STAGE 1: INITIAL CONSULTATION & DISCOVERY (Day 1-3)
===================================================

Purpose: Understand the client's business, goals, and target audience

Week 1 - Initial Meeting (Day 1)
-------------------------------
Format: 30-45 minute discovery call (video)

Topics Covered:
1. Business Overview
   - What does your business do?
   - How long have you been in business?
   - Current team size and structure
   - Annual revenue and growth trajectory

2. Sales & Lead Process
   - How do customers currently find you?
   - What's your current sales process?
   - How long is your sales cycle?
   - What's your average customer value?
   - What's your close rate (leads to sales)?

3. Target Customer
   - Who is your ideal customer?
   - Where are they located?
   - What's their age range?
   - What problems does your business solve?
   - Why do customers choose you over competitors?

4. Marketing History
   - What marketing have you tried before?
   - What worked? What didn't work?
   - Current marketing budget allocation
   - Any previous advertising experience?
   - Current social media presence (followers, activity level)

5. Goals & Expectations
   - What's your primary goal? (leads, traffic, sales)
   - What's your target lead volume per month?
   - What's your budget for Facebook/Instagram ads?
   - What timeline are you working with?
   - How will you measure success?

6. Logistics
   - Preferred communication method and frequency
   - Who is the primary point of contact?
   - Who else from their team should be involved?
   - Any technical access/permissions needed?

Deliverables:
- Client information form (completed)
- Initial assessment notes
- Schedule for next meetings

Initial Assessment Output:
- Project overview document
- High-level strategy outline
- Next steps and timeline

---

STAGE 2: STRATEGY & PLANNING (Day 4-10)
========================================

Purpose: Develop detailed strategy and gain client approval

Market Research (Internal)
---------------------------
Our team conducts independent research including:

1. Industry Analysis
   - Industry trends and growth patterns
   - Seasonal trends and opportunities
   - Common customer pain points
   - Average pricing and customer lifetime value

2. Competitor Analysis
   - Identify 5-10 main competitors
   - Analyze their Facebook/Instagram ads
   - Review their messaging and positioning
   - Identify market gaps and opportunities
   - Note their follower counts and engagement rates

3. Audience Research
   - Estimate addressable market size
   - Research demographics of ideal customers
   - Identify interest categories and behaviors
   - Find lookalike audience opportunities
   - Analyze search trends related to their services

4. Platform Assessment
   - Review their current social media presence
   - Assess website quality and conversion potential
   - Evaluate existing content
   - Identify technical setup needs

Competitive Mapping Document
- 3-5 competitors listed
- Their messaging and positioning
- Their ad strategies and creative types
- Pricing comparison
- Gap analysis (what client can do better)

Strategy Session with Client (Day 7-8)
---------------------------------------
Format: 60-90 minute strategy call (video)

Agenda:
1. Research Findings Presentation (20 mins)
   - Market overview
   - Competitor landscape
   - Opportunity identification
   - Target audience deep dive

2. Proposed Campaign Strategy (30 mins)
   - Primary campaign objectives
   - Target audience segments
   - Campaign structure overview
   - Expected performance benchmarks
   - Creative strategy (messaging angles)
   - Timeline and milestones

3. Q&A and Discussion (20 mins)
   - Client questions and concerns
   - Refinements to strategy
   - Pricing confirmation
   - Timeline confirmation

4. Next Steps (10 mins)
   - Tasks for client
   - Tasks for GrowthForge
   - Timeline for campaign launch
   - Communication schedule

Strategy Document Delivered:
- Executive Summary (1 page)
- Market Analysis (2-3 pages)
- Target Audience Profile (1-2 pages)
- Campaign Strategy (2-3 pages)
- Success Metrics & KPIs (1 page)
- 90-Day Roadmap (1 page)
- Pricing & Investment (1 page)

STAGE 3: TECHNICAL SETUP (Day 11-16)
====================================

Purpose: Set up all systems, accounts, and tracking

Account Creation & Setup
------------------------
Completed by GrowthForge Team:

1. Facebook/Instagram Ad Account
   - Create Business Manager (if needed)
   - Create Ad Account
   - Set permissions and access
   - Configure billing settings
   - Add client as account owner

2. Pixel Installation
   - Facebook Pixel installation on website
   - Conversion tracking setup
   - Standard event tracking configured
   - Custom conversion creation
   - Quality check and testing

3. CRM Integration
   - Email integration setup (if applicable)
   - Lead form configuration
   - Email sequence automation (if applicable)
   - Data syncing testing
   - Lead routing configuration

4. Analytics Setup
   - Google Analytics 4 configuration (if not already done)
   - Facebook Pixel verified
   - Conversion tracking confirmed
   - UTM parameter setup
   - Custom dashboard creation

5. Asset Preparation
   - Logo files collected and organized
   - Brand colors and guidelines documented
   - Product/service images gathered
   - Testimonial requests sent to client
   - Video content sourced or planned

Client Action Items Due:
- Brand guidelines and assets
- Product/service images (10-15 high quality)
- Website access and passwords
- CRM/email system access
- Testimonials or case studies
- Copy approval for landing pages (if using funnels)

Technical Setup Checklist:
✓ Ad account created and configured
✓ Pixel installed and tracking verified
✓ Conversion tracking working
✓ CRM integration tested
✓ Analytics properly configured
✓ Dashboard access provided to client
✓ All team members with access trained

STAGE 4: CREATIVE DEVELOPMENT (Day 11-16)
=========================================

Purpose: Create ad creative and copy that converts

Creative Development Process:

1. Messaging Framework
   - Primary value proposition
   - Key benefits (3-5 main points)
   - Unique selling points
   - Common objections to address
   - Call-to-action strategy

2. Ad Creative Types Planned:
   - Carousel ads (3-5 cards each)
   - Single image ads (2-3 variations)
   - Video ads (if applicable)
   - Collection ads (if product-based)
   - Lead form ads (if using direct lead capture)

3. Copy Development:
   - Headlines (3-5 variations per ad)
   - Body copy (attention-grabbing, benefit-focused)
   - Call-to-action (clear and compelling)
   - Ad text optimization for mobile
   - Landing page copy (if using funnels)

4. Design & Production:
   - Graphic design using brand assets
   - Video editing (if video ads used)
   - Image optimization for platform
   - A/B testing variations
   - Thumbnail creation for video ads

5. Client Review & Approval:
   - Present initial creative concepts
   - Gather feedback and revisions
   - Refine based on feedback
   - Final approval before launch
   - 2 rounds of revisions included

Creative Deliverables:
- 3-5 ad variations ready for testing
- Copy variations for A/B testing
- Landing page (if applicable)
- Ad schedule and rotation plan
- Creative testing roadmap (for months 2-3)

Creative Brief Provided:
- Target audience persona
- Campaign objective
- Primary messaging angle
- Supporting messages
- Call-to-action strategy
- Design specifications
- Performance benchmarks

---

STAGE 5: ACCOUNT CONFIGURATION (Day 16-19)
===========================================

Purpose: Configure campaigns, ad sets, and targeting

Campaign Architecture:

Level 1: Campaign
- Objective: Lead Generation or Conversions
- Budget: [Total monthly ad budget]
- Optimization: Configured for Lead Volume or ROAS

Level 2: Ad Sets
- One ad set per audience segment
- Audience targeting configured
- Geographic targeting set
- Device targeting configured
- Bid strategy selected
- Budget allocation set
- Schedule configured (if not 24/7)

Level 3: Ads
- 3-5 different ad creative variations
- Different copy versions
- Headlines and descriptions
- Links and landing pages
- Call-to-action buttons

Audience Configuration:

Primary Audiences:
1. Core Audience (Interest-based)
   - Key interests defined
   - Behaviors targeted
   - Demographics narrowed
   - Age range specified
   - Geographic radius set
   - Excluded audiences configured

2. Lookalike Audience (if available)
   - Based on customer list or website visitors
   - 1% lookalike (most similar)
   - 2% lookalike (second tier)
   - 5% lookalike (broader reach)
   - Geographic targeting applied

3. Retargeting Audience (if applicable)
   - Website visitors (last 30 days)
   - Past customer list
   - Email list engagement
   - Lead magnet downloaders

Budget & Bidding:

Bid Strategy:
- Automatic CPC (Cost Per Click) - for traffic
- Target Cost (Lead Generation) - for leads
- Manual Bidding - for advanced optimization
- Bid cap if needed

Budget Allocation:
- Daily budget vs. Lifetime budget
- Seasonal adjustments
- Scaling plan (how to increase over time)
- Testing budget (small amount for new audiences)
- Proven winner budget (larger allocation)

Testing Allocation:
- 20% of budget to new variations/audiences
- 80% of budget to proven winners
- Weekly reallocation based on performance

Tracking & Measurement Setup:

Conversion Tracking:
- Website lead form submissions
- Phone call clicks
- Messenger inquiries
- Purchase completions (e-commerce)
- Custom event tracking

Reporting Setup:
- Daily performance monitoring
- Lead tracking system
- Cost per lead calculation
- Quality score tracking
- Attribution tracking

Configuration Verification Checklist:
✓ Campaign structure matches strategy
✓ Ad sets properly segmented
✓ Audiences correctly configured
✓ Targeting matches strategy
✓ Bid strategy appropriate for goals
✓ Budget allocation matches plan
✓ Tracking properly configured
✓ Exclusions in place
✓ Ad schedules set (if applicable)
✓ Mobile optimization enabled

Performance Testing:

STAGE 7: CAMPAIGN LAUNCH (Day 21)
==================================

Purpose: Go live and begin lead generation

Launch Day Activities:

Pre-Launch (Morning)
- Final QA check
- Confirm client availability for questions
- Set up real-time monitoring
- Prepare launch summary document
- Verify all systems are operational

Campaign Launch (Scheduled Time)
- Activate all ad sets
- Confirm ads are serving
- Monitor initial delivery
- Check for any technical issues
- Verify tracking is working

Post-Launch (First 2 hours)
- Monitor ad delivery and engagement
- Check click quality and early performance
- Respond to any immediate technical issues
- Communicate with client confirmation
- Take screenshots for documentation
- Prepare initial performance report

Launch Day Deliverables:
- Campaign launch confirmation email
- Live dashboard link and instructions
- Performance monitoring guide
- First 24-hour performance summary
- Next week's action plan
- Emergency contact numbers

Client Communication:
✓ Confirmation that campaigns are live
✓ Where to view performance data
✓ When to expect first leads
✓ How to forward leads for optimization data
✓ Schedule for first check-in call
✓ How to contact if issues arise

---

STAGE 8: FIRST WEEK MONITORING & ADJUSTMENT (Day 22-28)
========================================================

Purpose: Monitor performance and make initial optimizations

Daily Activities (First 7 Days):

Day 1-2:
- Monitor delivery and costs
- Check for technical issues
- Early performance assessment
- Adjust bids if delivery too fast/slow
- Collect client feedback

Day 3-5:
- Initial lead quality assessment
- Gauge real-world conversion rates
- Identify any targeting issues
- Compare performance to benchmarks
- Make first optimizations

Day 6-7:
- 7-day performance review
- Early results summary
- Plan optimizations for week 2
- Prepare client update

First Week Check-in Call (Day 5-7)
-----------------------------------
30-minute call with client

Topics:
1. Initial Performance Review (10 mins)
   - Total spend and leads
   - Cost per lead vs. target
   - Lead quality feedback from client
   - Any technical issues

2. Optimization Insights (10 mins)
   - What's working well
   - What needs adjustment
   - Proposed changes for week 2
   - Expected improvements

3. Feedback & Questions (10 mins)
   - Client questions and concerns
   - Lead quality assessment
   - Any missing information needed
   - Adjustments to strategy

First Week Deliverables:
- Daily performance updates (email)
- 7-day performance summary
- Initial optimization insights
- Proposed adjustments for week 2
- Client feedback summary

Initial Optimization Decisions:
- Which ads to expand budget on
- Which audiences are performing
- Which variations to pause or expand
- Bid adjustments (if needed)
- New audiences to test
- Creative variations to test next

=============================

Day 1-3: Initial Discovery & Consultation
Day 4-10: Strategy Development & Planning
Day 11-16: Technical Setup & Creative Development
Day 16-19: Campaign Configuration
Day 19-20: QA & Testing
Day 21: Campaign Launch
Day 22-28: First Week Monitoring

Total Onboarding Time: 3 weeks

Success Criteria for Completed Onboarding:
✓ Client fully trained and autonomous with dashboard
✓ Campaigns generating qualified leads
✓ Tracking working and reporting accurate
✓ Initial optimization cycle completed
✓ Client expectations aligned with reality
✓ Weekly process and communication established
✓ 30-60 day forecast prepared
✓ Success metrics clearly defined

Typical First Month Performance (by Week):
Week 1-2: Testing phase, collecting data, higher CPA
Week 3: Optimization showing results, CPA declining
Week 4: Stable performance, ready for scaling
Month 2+: Optimized campaigns, consistent lead flow

The goal of onboarding is not just to launch campaigns, but to launch your success in a structured, scalable way.

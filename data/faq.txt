PRICING QUESTIONS
=================

Q: How much do your services cost?
A: Pricing is based on two components:
1. Management Fee: Based on your ad spend budget ($250-$1,500/month depending on scale)
2. Ad Spend: The actual cost of running ads (paid to Facebook, separate from our fee)

For example, if you allocate $2,000/month for ads, you'd pay:
- Ad spend: $2,000
- Management fee: ~$600
- Total: $2,600/month

Q: What's your minimum investment?
A: $250/month management fee minimum, plus at least $500-$1,000/month in ad spend. We recommend starting at $1,000-$1,500/month for meaningful results.

Q: Is the management fee negotiable?
A: On packages under $2,500/month ad spend, pricing is set. For $5,000+/month, we can discuss custom pricing and terms.

Q: Can I pause or cancel anytime?
A: For Starter packages, you can cancel with 30 days notice. Growth and Premium packages have 60-90 day cancellation notice recommended. There are no early termination fees - we'd rather keep you happy by delivering results.

Q: What if I can't afford a large ad budget to start?
A: We recommend starting with at least $1,000/month to generate enough data for optimization. If that's too much, you might not be ready for paid ads yet. Focus on organic growth first.

Q: Do I pay the management fee if campaigns aren't running?
A: If there's an issue on our end, no. If you pause campaigns by choice, the fee still applies since we're maintaining the account and strategy. Usually only comes up during seasonal breaks.

Q: Can you work with my existing ad account?
A: Yes, we can take over management of existing campaigns. We'll audit everything first and usually recommend pausing old campaigns to start fresh with our optimized structure.

RESULTS & PERFORMANCE QUESTIONS
================================

Q: How many leads should I expect?
A: Depends on your industry and budget:
- Local services ($1,500/month budget): 60-100 leads
- Professional services: 30-50 leads
- Fitness: 60-100 leads
- Real estate: 25-40 leads
- Each industry varies based on competition and cost per lead

Q: How long until I see results?
A: Week 1: Ads are live, data is being collected
Week 2-3: Initial trends visible, first optimizations happening
Week 4: Stable performance emerging
Month 2: Significant optimization improvements (typically 20-30% cost reduction)
Month 3+: Optimal performance and ready to scale

Q: What's a good cost per lead?
A: Varies by industry:
- Local services: $12-18
- Professional services: $15-25
- Fitness: $8-15
- Real estate: $20-40

If your customer lifetime value is high, $30-40/lead can still be profitable.

Q: How much will it cost to generate a customer?
A: Cost Per Lead ÷ Close Rate = Cost Per Customer

Example: $15 CPL ÷ 20% close rate = $75 cost per customer
If your average customer is worth $1,500, that's a 20:1 return.

Q: Can you guarantee a specific ROI?
A: No, but we work hard to ensure your cost per lead is as low as possible while maintaining quality. Your ROI depends on your sales process and close rate, which we don't control. However, we DO track and report on the quality of leads we generate.

Q: What if leads aren't converting?
A: Common causes:
1. Sales process issues (not calling back quickly, poor pitch)
2. Lead quality being misidentified (they're less qualified than expected)
3. Offer doesn't match what's being promised in ads
4. Timing issues (calling during closed hours, etc.)

We can adjust ad targeting and messaging, but ultimately your close rate is your responsibility. We can help by collecting lead feedback.

Q: Can you help improve my close rate?
A: We can provide coaching, but it's not our primary service. We focus on lead quality and volume. We can help by:
- Gathering lead feedback from your sales team
- Adjusting ad targeting to pre-qualify better
- Testing different offers
- Connecting with higher-intent audiences

Q: What if I get bad leads?
A: If leads are below expected quality, we:
1. Adjust targeting to be more specific
2. Change messaging to pre-qualify better
3. Test different audience segments
4. Add qualification questions to lead forms
5. Expand budget to winners, pause underperformers

Most quality issues are solved in week 2-3 of optimization.

Q: How do you measure lead quality?
A: We measure what we can control:
- Click quality (relevant traffic vs. accidental clicks)
- Form completion rate (indicates real interest)
- Lead form data accuracy (valid contact info)
- Engagement patterns (engaged leads behave differently)

You measure what you control:
- Conversion rate (leads to customers)
- Deal size from those customers
- Customer quality (repeat business, referrals, lifetime value)

We share data to help you identify trends.

Q: Will leads continue after I stop paying?
A: No. Facebook Ads stops running when campaigns are paused. Any leads in process will continue in your sales pipeline, but no new leads are generated. This is different from organic social, where content continues to work.

Q: Can I scale my lead volume if things are working?
A: Yes. Once campaigns are optimized and profitable, we can increase budget 10-20% at a time to scale leads. Most clients scale 2-3x by month 3-4.

COMPARISON & COMPETITIVE QUESTIONS
===================================

Q: How are you different from other agencies?
A: Key differences:
1. Local Business Focus: We specialize in local/service businesses, not general e-commerce
2. Lead Generation: We focus on qualified leads, not followers or engagement
3. Transparency: You see exactly what's working and costs
4. Guarantee: If we miss targets after 90 days, we work at 50% fee until we hit them
5. Results: 150+ clients with 3-5x lead volume improvement

Q: Why not use Google Ads?
A: Google Ads is best for:
- Search intent (people searching for your service)
- Competitive keywords (often expensive)
- Immediate needs

Facebook Ads is best for:
- Local targeting
- Awareness (finding people who don't know about you)
- Behavioral targeting (finding your ideal customer)
- Relationship building (nurturing leads)

Best answer: Use both. But Facebook usually has lower CPL.

Q: How do you compare to freelance ad managers?
A: Freelancers often:
- Manage many clients simultaneously
- Limited strategic oversight
- May not optimize consistently
- Less accountability

We offer:
- Dedicated strategy and optimization
- Team expertise (not one person)
- Proven systems and processes
- Results-based accountability

Q: Why not do this ourselves?
A: You could. But:
- Learning curve (3-6 months to get proficient)
- Time commitment (10-15 hours/week minimum)
- Skill requirements (strategy, psychology, design)
- Data analysis (interpreting results)
- Continuous learning (algorithm changes, platform updates)

Most business owners make more money focusing on their business than learning ads.

Q: When should I hire an agency vs. doing it myself?
A: Hire an agency when:
- Your hourly rate is worth more than learning ads
- You want professional results faster
- You need accountability and expertise
- Your marketing budget is $1,000+/month
- You want to scale quickly

Do it yourself when:
- Learning is enjoyable for you
- You have 10+ hours/week to learn and manage
- Your budget is under $500/month
- You want full control
- You're comfortable with a slow learning curve
